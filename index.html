Introduction

The contemplation of extraterrestrial life and the exploration of exoplanets, celestial bodies orbiting stars beyond our solar system, has long been a controversial topic.

Controversy Surrounding UFO Sightings

Media often portrays extraterrestrial life as UFO sightings, however, there have not been solid evidence proving these are real. Such occurrences can impact advancement as it may divert resources from legitimate scientific endeavours. It has been proven that these encounters are also easily faked. One such example is when Tom Callen, a producer for Cosmonova, took fake sightings of UFOs (Callen, 2003). Even with the limited technology in the early 2000s, Callen was still able to produce convincing fakes. With the more advanced photo editing, generative AI, and just equipment overall, it is extremely easy to deceive people. Evidence of Extraterrestrial Life:

There have been promising evidence suggesting that there is life beyond Earth or at least is capable of inhabiting life. One of these being the discovery of certain sugars such as ribose in meteorites. Ribose is a key building block for RNA which is important because meteorites might have brought organic molecules, like sugars, to early Earth. The presence of these extraterrestrial sugars suggests they could have played a role in creating important compounds like RNA on our planet. This suggests that there may be other planets like earth that have formed similar compounds (Yoshihiro et al, 2019). However, these compounds would not mean anything if there were not the right living conditions.
